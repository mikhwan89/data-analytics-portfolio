Live Project
Data doesn't just describe reality — it can be structured to argue a point.

mystoreofvalue.com is a financial analytics platform I built that tracks long-term asset performance across cryptocurrencies, commodities, and market indices. But it's more than a data display — it's an argument. The site is designed to take someone who asks "what should I hold to protect my wealth?" and walk them, step by step, through the evidence that leads to a clear, data-backed answer. Every metric shown, every chart drawn, every ranking presented serves that narrative.

Visit mystoreofvalue.com → Live at mystoreofvalue.com
Mechanism 01
The question leads the data

The site opens with a question real investors ask: which assets actually hold value over time? Every chart and table that follows is the answer to that question — not just data for its own sake.

Mechanism 02
Metrics chosen to persuade

CAGR, Sharpe ratio, max drawdown, and Sortino ratio aren't shown because they exist — each one is selected because it exposes a different dimension of the argument. Risk-adjusted returns tell a much stronger story than raw gains alone.

Mechanism 03
Rankings close the argument

The leaderboard lets the data state the conclusion itself. Visitors don't need to be told which assets win — sorting the table by any metric makes the answer self-evident. The analyst's job is done before the user even scrolls.
